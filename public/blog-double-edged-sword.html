The Double-Edged Sword: Technology and Cybercrime

March 15, 2023 Cybersecurity 6 min read

In today's world, where technology plays a major role, almost everyone owns modern devices. Technology has brought us so forward in our life that even the older generation can compete with the tech-savvy youth. However, this technological advancement comes with its own set of challenges and dilemmas.

It is often asked by many parents whether introducing the concept of technology to children is a boon or a bane. While technology offers incredible educational opportunities and connectivity, it has also led to a major disadvantage: with the help of advanced scientific knowledge, hackers are exploiting vulnerabilities in devices and illegally accessing personal information.

The Dark Side of the Internet

The internet and web technologies have been highly honored for revolutionizing communication and information access. However, there still seems to be a dark side of the internet world that has emerged. This mainly involves spam, malware, phishing attacks, and other malicious activities.

"Hacking is an attempt to exploit a computer system or a private network inside a computer for illicit purposes."

Hacking represents unauthorized access to computer network security systems. The most common cybercrime is hacking, where individuals gain access to other people's computers and passwords to use them for their own wrongful gain.

What is Cybercrime?

Cybercrime is the use of computers and networks to perform illegal activities such as spreading computer viruses, online bullying, performing unauthorized electronic fund transfers, and more. Some cybercrimes can also be carried out using mobile phones via SMS and online chatting applications.

Cybercrimes can be regarded as offences that are committed against individuals or groups of individuals with a criminal motive to intentionally harm the reputation of the victim or cause physical or mental harm, or loss, to the victim directly or indirectly.

What is Ethical Hacking?

Another topic about which everyone is curious to know is ethical hacking. Basically, it is an authorized practice of bypassing system security to identify potential threats in a network. The company that owns the system or network allows cybersecurity engineers to perform such activities to test the system's defenses. Thus, unlike malicious hacking, this process is planned, approved, and crucially, legal.

By conducting penetration tests, an ethical hacker looks to answer the following four basic questions:

  • What information/locations/systems can an attacker gain access to?
  • What can an attacker see on the target?
  • What can an attacker do with available information?
  • Does anyone at the target system notice the attempts?

The Role of Ethical Hackers

An ethical hacker operates with the knowledge and permission of the organization for which they are trying to defend. In some cases, the organization will neglect to inform their information security team of the activities that will be carried out by an ethical hacker in an attempt to test the effectiveness of the information security team. This is referred to as a double-blind environment.

Ethical hacking is mainly focused on fighting against terrorism and national security breaches. It is used to build systems that help prevent the penetration of secretive information by malicious hackers. Moreover, it provides security to banking and financial establishments.

Conclusion

In conclusion, cybercrime is designed for the disadvantageous purpose of exploiting citizens, whereas ethical hacking is organized under particular groups or specific organizations for protective purposes. While cybercrime seeks to harm and exploit, ethical hacking works to create a safer digital environment for everyone.

As technology continues to evolve, the line between these two facets of cybersecurity will become increasingly important to understand. The future of digital security depends on our ability to harness the skills of ethical hackers to protect against those with malicious intent.
